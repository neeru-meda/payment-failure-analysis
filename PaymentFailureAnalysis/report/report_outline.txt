Empirical analysis of 10,000 synthetic transactions reveals that 
payment failure incidence rates exhibit statistically significant 
temporal clustering. Transactions initiated during the 10pm–1am 
core banking system (CBS) maintenance window demonstrate a 2.95x 
elevation in failure incidence (45.73%) relative to the system 
baseline (15.52%), while salary credit days (28th–31st of month) 
produce a 2.25x spike attributed to peak concurrent load on 
NPCI settlement infrastructure. These findings indicate material 
SLA compliance risk for banks operating under RBI's mandated 
99.5% payment availability standard during high-volume windows.
